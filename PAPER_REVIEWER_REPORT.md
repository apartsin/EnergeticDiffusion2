# Referee Report: DGLD: Domain-Gated Latent Diffusion for the Discovery of Novel Energetic Materials

**Journal:** Nature Machine Intelligence
**Manuscript:** docs/paper/index.html (Aperstein, Berman, Apartsin)
**Reviewer role:** Diffusion-models / energetic-materials ML

---

## 1. Summary in reviewer's words

The authors propose a multi-stage molecular generator for high-energy-density CHNO materials. A pretrained LIMO VAE is fine-tuned on a 326 k energetic-flavoured corpus and frozen; a 44.6 M-parameter conditional DDPM is trained over the 1024-d latent under classifier-free guidance, with a four-tier label-trust mask that lets only experimental and DFT labels drive the conditional gradient. A separately trained noise-conditional multi-head score model adds per-head viability/sensitivity/hazard/SA gradients at sample time. Generated candidates pass through a chemistry-credibility ladder: chemist SMARTS rules, GFN2-xTB HOMO-LUMO triage, B3LYP/6-31G(d) optimisation calibrated by two anchors (RDX, TATB), then an AiZynthFinder retrosynthesis check. The headline lead, trinitro-1,2-isoxazole, is reported as PubChem-novel with predicted rho=2.00, D=9.56, P=40.5 GPa, a real DFT minimum, and reachable in four retrosynthetic steps. The genuine contribution is methodological: (i) a tier-stratified label-mask recipe; (ii) the diagnosis and one-line fix of an alpha-anneal bug that silently mutes classifier guidance in small-step latent diffusion; (iii) an end-to-end credibility funnel run on commodity hardware. The chemistry result is candidate-grade only.

## 2. Strengths

**A clean methodology contribution backed by a clean ablation.** The §5.3.2 four-condition diagnostic (alpha-anneal on/off, clamp 5 vs 50; bit-identical MD5 within each anneal setting) is the most interesting result in the paper. The alpha-anneal silently nullifying classifier guidance at 40 steps is a real, reproducible failure mode of the standard image-domain recipe ported to molecular latent diffusion, and the authors document it with the kind of MD5 forensics other groups can act on. Appendix E.5's diagnostic protocol (per-step gradient norm, score-model output sensitivity, final-z cosine similarity, decoder argmax-basin width) is the right artefact to release.

**Tier-stratified label gating is a sensible response to the small-noisy-label regime.** Most prior energetic-materials ML work either pools tiers (degrading calibration) or trains only on Tier-A (~3000 rows, insufficient). Restricting the conditional gradient to A+B while letting C and D shape the unconditional prior via cfg-dropout is a defensible compromise, and the recipe transfers to other tier-stratified domains (the photovoltaic / redox-flow / opioid-sparing analogue is plausible).

**The baseline ladder is honestly framed and informative.** Showing that a same-corpus SMILES-LSTM achieves Tanimoto = 1.000 to a labelled-master row (memorisation) and that MolMIM 70 M produces novel chemistry at D = 7.70 km/s isolates what diffusion + guidance contribute. The Phase-A composite is correctly flagged as not a cross-family metric, and the paper does not try to claim FCD wins.

**Validation chain runs end-to-end on cheap hardware.** Sampling fits in ~8-25 minutes on a rented RTX 4090; DFT validation of the 12 chem-pass leads + 2 anchors + 3 reference-class scaffolds completed under modest GPU budget via gpu4pyscf. Compute-budget transparency in Appendix E.2 is unusual and welcome. The paper genuinely demonstrates that the credibility gate can be assembled from public components.

**The §5.5 K-J-vs-3D-CNN residual analysis is rigorous.** Carrying the residual decomposition out to a 575-row Tier-A population test (Pearson r(f_N, residual) = +0.43, p < 1e-26, table D.4) and explicitly recovering the known K-J failure mode at high N-fraction is the right level of self-criticism. The "ranking-grade rather than absolute-grade" framing is honest.

## 3. Major weaknesses

**(a) Two-anchor DFT calibration is statistically unjustifiable for the headline number.** The §5.5 calibration fits rho_cal = 4.275 * rho_DFT - 5.172 and HOF_cal = HOF_DFT - 16763 kJ/mol from RDX and TATB only. (b) Two points fix slope and intercept exactly with zero residual diagnostic, the slope of 4.275 is implausibly steep, and a single packing-coefficient choice of 0.69 (range 0.65-0.78) shifts rho_cal by ±0.15 g/cm³, which dominates any per-lead claim. The paper acknowledges this but still reports L1 calibrated rho = 2.53 g/cm³ in Table D.1, a number higher than every CHNO compound in regular use including CL-20 (1.99) and ONC (2.06). The headline rho = 2.00 g/cm³ for L1 in §5.2 is the 3D-CNN surrogate output, not the DFT calibrate, so the abstract conflates surrogate and DFT-anchored numbers. (c) What would convince me: a 6-anchor calibration (HMX, PETN, FOX-7, NTO added) with leave-one-out residual standard deviations on rho and HOF, plus a bootstrap on the K-J recompute. The Appendix D.4 acknowledgement of the 6-anchor extension as deferred is exactly what should have been done before submission.

**(a) The K-J recompute on calibrated DFT inputs produces ranking residuals of +0.7 to +4.3 km/s, with three of nine leads (L1, L5, L19, including the headline) returning "K-J undefined" because the calibrated HOF crosses the closed-form sign convention.** (b) For the headline candidate L1, the paper's headline D = 9.56 km/s comes from the 3D-CNN surrogate, not from any DFT-anchored thermochemical computation. Once calibrated DFT is used, the K-J branch is undefined; once raw DFT is used, K-J gives D = 13.18 km/s, a number neither the authors nor any reader would defend. The paper therefore has no DFT-anchored detonation-velocity number for L1 at all, only a surrogate one. (c) An EXPLO5 or Cheetah-2 recompute on the calibrated DFT inputs is required before the abstract's "predicted physical properties match the strongest explosives in regular use" sentence can stand. The current evidence supports "predicted by a 3D-CNN trained on a partially-overlapping corpus to be HMX-class," which is markedly weaker.

**(a) The novelty claim against the augmented training corpus rests on a Morgan-FP-2 Tanimoto window that is too generous.** (b) The Tanimoto window (0.20, 0.55) is reported as "close enough to be plausible, far enough to be novel," but Morgan-FP-2 with 2048 bits and radius 2 is known to over-discriminate on small CHNO heterocycles where bit-collisions are rare; a Tanimoto of 0.55 corresponds to chemically very-similar molecules in this regime (different N-positions on the same ring scaffold). The "0/100 within Tanimoto 0.70" is therefore a weak novelty bound; the relevant question is scaffold novelty, and Table D.5 shows scaf-NN-mean of 0.42-0.47 with a max of 1.000 across all guidance conditions. (c) Reporting Bemis-Murcko scaffold novelty against the augmented corpus, plus a ring-system-only Tanimoto on the merged top-100, would tighten the claim. The 24 unique BM scaffolds across the merged top-100 (§5.3.4) is the more useful number and should be foregrounded.

**(a) The "96/100 PubChem-novel" claim has not been audited for canonicalisation drift.** (b) PubChem PUG-REST on canonical SMILES misses tautomers, charge variants, and isotopologues with high probability for nitro-rich heterocycles, where multiple resonance-equivalent SMILES exist. The 1/97 hit (1-nitro-1H-tetrazol-1-amine) is described as a sanity-check rediscovery, but the 3 transient REST errors silently dropped from the denominator are a red flag: any robust novelty audit should retry these. (c) An InChIKey-level cross-check, or a query against the standard chemistry indices PubChem, ChEMBL, and Reaxys, would be the right standard. The paper does not say whether trinitro-1,2-isoxazole has been claimed in any patent or doctoral thesis, which is a non-trivial gap given the obvious chemistry of the scaffold.

**(a) The retrosynthetic claim for L1 is over-stated relative to the artefact.** (b) The paper's §5.6 narrative describes a 4-step Curtius / DPPA / Boc / nitration route from tert-butanol + 4,5-dinitro-1,2-isoxazole-3-carboxylic acid. The actual top route in aizynth_results.json begins with HNO3 + 4,5-dinitroisoxazole and is a single direct C-H nitration; the precursor is itself listed as not in stock (in_stock: false). The route is therefore (i) a one-step retro-disconnection of the headline molecule to a precursor whose own synthesis is not solved by AiZynthFinder, and (ii) the narrative description in the paper does not match the JSON. This is the kind of mismatch that gets a paper retracted from a chemistry venue. (c) Either the JSON or the prose has to be authoritative; the prose should be regenerated from the artefact and the precursor 4,5-dinitroisoxazole's own synthesis traced before claiming "reachable in four steps from public reaction templates."

**(a) The headline numbers rest on a single sampling stream with limited seed variance.** (b) §7 admits 89/100 of the merged top-100 come from one unguided pool=40 000 reranked by the Phase-A composite, with seed variance estimated only via a separate 3-seed pool=10 000 matrix. The denoiser itself was trained once (no seed variance on the model). For an NMI methodology paper, three independent denoiser trainings + three sampling seeds each would be the minimum standard; for a chemistry-claim paper, the absence of any wet-lab data means the headline candidates should at minimum be reproduced across sampling seeds and across two independent denoiser trainings. (c) Re-running the headline pipeline with at least three denoiser seeds and reporting top-1 stability over the full 9-cell grid would close this.

**(a) Multiple internal inconsistencies suggest the manuscript was assembled from heterogeneous late-stage results.** (b) The abstract reports rho = 2.00, D = 9.56, P = 40.5 (3D-CNN surrogate); §5.5 states L1 raw DFT rho = 1.80, calibrated rho = 2.53; the K-J recompute on calibrated DFT is undefined for L1; Table 1's row 1 shows ρ = 2.00, D = 9.56, P = 40.52, viab = 1.00, perf = 1.00 which is the unrealistic "everything equals 1" pattern that often signals saturated banded scoring. The §5.3.4 statement "the trinitro-isoxazole rank-1 itself comes from the guided run" contradicts §5.3.4's source-pool breakdown which attributes 89/100 (including, by implication, top-1) to the unguided pool. (c) A single results-consistency pass against m2_summary.json, m6_post.json, novelty_top1.json, and aizynth_results.json is required.

## 4. Minor issues

- §1, paragraph 2: "Detonation pressure P at the Chapman-Jouguet state" is correct but the paper later reports P numbers that are 3D-CNN surrogate outputs, not C-J states. Be explicit about provenance throughout.
- §1.5, contribution 5: "The fix is a one-line change (disable the anneal)" - but Appendix C.3 also recommends per-head gradient-norm rebalancing; it is unclear whether the headline numbers use rebalancing.
- §2.7: "We adopt MOSES-style metrics in Appendix E.11" - the validity rate of 1.000 across all conditions is suspicious; LIMO non-autoregressive decoders typically produce some unstrained / non-canonical outputs. Audit the validity check.
- §3.1, Table: Tier-A row count of "~3000" contradicts §5.5's "575 Tier-A rows of the labelled master that carry simultaneous experimental rho, HOF, D"; one of these numbers is wrong.
- §3.2: "validation token-accuracy is 64.5%" - this is a very low number for a fine-tuned VAE. Discuss whether this affects the round-trip fidelity claim of §6.
- §4.4: The hard-negative count (137 vs 918) is referenced multiple ways across §C.1, §E.9; the table in C.1 says "918 (v3d/v3e)" but §E.9 calls v3d the 918-version and the production v3e is also 918; the "additional aromatic-heterocycle boost x1.20" in v3d but not v3e adds to the confusion. Consolidate.
- §4.5, hard filter (vi): "≥3 nitro groups on a one- or two-carbon skeleton" - but Table 1 row 4 has 3 nitro groups on a 3-carbon skeleton with a NH-N bond; document the SMARTS exactly.
- §5.2 prose: "all five top leads have viability ≥ 0.99" - but Table 1 shows row 3 viab = 0.83, row 4 = 0.93, row 5 = 0.86. Inconsistent.
- §5.3.1: 3 PubChem REST errors should be retried, not dropped from the denominator. Standard practice.
- §5.4 Table 5: rank 4 verdict says "zwitterion appears on opt: unstable" - but the gap is 1.07 eV; was the verdict driven by the gap or the zwitterionisation? Separate the two.
- §5.5: HOF intercept of -16763 kJ/mol described as "well-known systematic atomization-energy bias of B3LYP/6-31G(d)" - the GMTKN55 5-15 kJ/mol per atom would predict ~150-300 kJ/mol for a 15-25 atom molecule, off by two orders of magnitude. The intercept absorbs the un-subtracted reference-state atomic energies (hartree); say so in plain language.
- Table D.2: row L9 K-J calibrated D = 7.51 vs raw DFT D = 11.69 vs 3D-CNN D = 9.27 - three numbers, three different stories. Pick one.
- Figure 12 caption: "Note on graph-survival false positives" - if this is significant enough to put in the caption, the underlying figure should be replaced rather than caveated.
- Appendix E.10: "top-1 composite 0.91 at pool=5000 but only 0.60-0.63 at pool=20000/40000" - this is the opposite of the §5.1 message that yield scales with pool size. Clarify whether top-1 composite is absolute or relative.
- Appendix E.12: hazard-head AUROC reported as 0.91 ± 0.05 from "5-fold cross-validation across all 196 hazard-dataset rows" but recall on the merged top-100 is reported as "perfect by construction"; spell out which rows were positive/negative training labels.
- Reference list: ref-kamlet1968detonation appears twice (entries 12 and 50 by ID, items 12 and 50 in the ordered list).

## 5. Specific questions for the authors

1. The headline trinitro-1,2-isoxazole D = 9.56 km/s is a 3D-CNN surrogate prediction, not a DFT-anchored thermochemistry. What is the EXPLO5 or Cheetah-2 D for this molecule using the calibrated DFT rho and HOF, and how does it compare?
2. Has trinitro-1,2-isoxazole been searched against ChEMBL, Reaxys, USPTO patents, and the SciFinder energetic-materials index, or only PubChem PUG-REST? If PubChem only, why?
3. The §5.6 prose describes a 4-step Curtius/Boc/nitration route. The aizynth_results.json shows a 1-step direct nitration of 4,5-dinitro-1,2-isoxazole. Reconcile.
4. Why does the K-J recompute return "undefined" for three of the nine DFT-converged leads, and what does this say about the calibration's validity in the oxygen-rich regime that the leads target?
5. Was the denoiser trained more than once? If yes, please report the across-seed top-1 spread on the merged top-100. If no, please retrain.
6. The viability head's training labels are 1[redflag pass] * P_RF(viable). This is a hard floor at the SMARTS pass; how does the gradient through the viability head behave in the noised-latent regions where most candidates have at least one polynitro motif? Does the gradient saturate at zero for most of the trajectory?
7. Why is FCD (Table 7) reported only against the labelled master and not against PubChem? FCD against the broader CHNO subset of PubChem would give a real distributional out-of-domain signal.
8. The Phase-A composite (§4.5) is bounded above by 1.0 in S_perf^band. Table 1's top row shows perf = 1.00 exactly. Is the headline candidate at the saturating boundary of the scoring function, and if so, what evidence is there that property predictions in this saturating regime are calibrated?
9. The ZINC-15 negatives used to train the viability classifier (80k random drug-like molecules, MW < 500) are a peculiar choice given the energetic chemistry domain. Why not ChEMBL, or unlabelled rows from the augmented energetic corpus?
10. What is the experimental strategy for L1? An aromatic trinitro-isoxazole is plausible chemistry; have the authors approached a synthetic group? An "in collaboration with X group" footnote would substantially raise the credibility of the chemistry claim.

## 6. Reproducibility check

The Zenodo deposit (DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19821953) is reserved but described as "draft" with "the DOI mints on publication"; reviewers cannot currently inspect the artefact. NMI policy requires the deposit to be live and reviewer-accessible at submission time.

The on-disk artefact is partial: m2_summary.json contains 10 leads (L1, L4, L5, L9, L11, L13, L16, L18, L19, L20) but L2 and L3 are absent and the paper acknowledges this; m6_postprocess_bundle/results/ is empty (the m6_post.json referenced repeatedly as the source of the "best top-1 D = 9.72 km/s (Hz-C1 viab seed 1)" claim is not present in the bundle the reviewer can inspect). The headline retrosynthetic narrative does not match aizynth_results.json. The full hyperparameter grid is acknowledged as not reported (§7).

Configuration documentation is reasonable: Appendix C.1-C.3 specifies hard-negative counts, sensitivity-head training targets, and the optional unit-norm rebalancing. CFG scale (7), DDIM steps (40), cfg-dropout (0.10), property-dropout (0.30), Tanimoto window (0.20, 0.55) are all stated. Per-experiment compute budgets are documented in Appendix E.2.

Reproducibility gaps against the NMI checklist:
- No held-out test split (acknowledged; defensible for a candidate-discovery paper but should be flagged).
- No cross-validation on the multi-head score model viability head (E.12 reports 5-fold AUROC on hazard only).
- Single denoiser training; no seed variance on the model itself.
- Anchor-calibration sensitivity to packing coefficient not bootstrapped.
- The MOSES-style table reports validity = 1.000 for all conditions, which warrants an audit.

## 7. Decision recommendation to the editor

**Major revision.**

The methodology contributions in §4.4, §5.3.2, and the four-tier label-mask recipe are real and worth publishing. The infrastructure release (LIMO + multi-head score model + chemistry credibility ladder + DFT pipeline on commodity hardware) is a useful artefact for the community. However, the chemistry claim (trinitro-1,2-isoxazole at HMX-class properties, four-step retrosynthesis, novel-and-on-target headline) is currently unsupported at the level NMI requires: the calibrated DFT thermochemistry returns "K-J undefined" for the headline lead (§3 weakness 2), the 2-anchor calibration is statistically inadequate (§3 weakness 1), the retrosynthetic prose does not match the artefact (§3 weakness 5), and the headline rests on a single sampling stream and a single denoiser training (§3 weakness 6). These are substantive but addressable: a 6-anchor calibration with bootstrapped uncertainty, an EXPLO5 or Cheetah-2 recompute on calibrated DFT inputs for L1, an audited retrosynthesis narrative, a second denoiser training seed, and a results-consistency pass against the JSON artefacts would convert this from a methodology-with-shaky-chemistry-claims paper into a methodology-with-credible-chemistry-candidates paper. With those changes I would support acceptance; without them, the chemistry claim cannot stand.

## 8. Confidential comments to the editor

The diffusion-anneal bug-fix is the kind of small, sharp methodology contribution NMI publishes well, and the authors are honest about the limitations of their thermochemistry. My concern is that the headline rests on a chemistry result that no chemist on the editorial board will defend without an EXPLO5 or experimental anchor, and the manuscript shows enough late-stage stitching (mismatched retrosynthesis prose vs JSON, "everything equals 1" rows in Table 1, three different D values for L9 across surrogate/raw-DFT/calibrated-DFT) that I want a careful revision before printing. Compared to recent NMI molecular-generation papers (Hoogeboom EDM, MolMIM, DiGress), the methodology bar is met; the chemistry-validation bar is not yet, and energetic materials is exactly the domain where reviewer skepticism on extrapolation is highest. I would lean toward asking the authors to either reframe as a methodology paper with candidate-grade chemistry (which they are within one revision of doing) or anchor the chemistry with a proper thermochemical recompute before publication.
