**Reviewer persona:** Senior energetic-materials chemist (synthesis, DSC/TGA, h50/ESD, B3LYP/G2/CBS-QB3 thermochemistry, EXPLO5/Cheetah)
**Manuscript:** docs/paper/index.html (revision since prereview-v1.0)

## Section 1: Summary in chemist's words

The same DGLD pipeline produces the same headline lead (L1, 3,4,5-trinitro-1,2-isoxazole) but the underlying thermochemistry has moved substantially since v1.0. A unit-conversion bug in the DFT pipeline (ZPE read as Hartree rather than computed from wavenumbers; commit 4abe43d) inflated every cached HOF by roughly 15,600 kJ/mol; on patching it the calibration intercept dropped from -16,763 kJ/mol to -205.7 kJ/mol, the experimental anchor RDX HOF of +66 to +70 kJ/mol is now bracketed cleanly (DFT atomization-energy +240.4 kJ/mol minus the 205.7 offset gives +34.7 kJ/mol, within the 2-anchor LOO residual band of plus or minus 62.5 kJ/mol of experiment), and the K-J recompute produces finite values for all twelve chem-pass leads, including the previously undefined L1, L5, L19. The L4 = 13.27 km/s number that I flagged as physically implausible was a regression artefact and now reads 7.01 km/s under the corrected calibration. The authors added a free-route Cantera ideal-gas CJ recompute (commit 149852a) which honestly does not replace EXPLO5 but does kill the L4-above-CL-20 reading at the relative-ranking level. Per-lead h50 sensitivity numbers are now in Table 1 and Table D.1c (commit a7dace2), and the L1 novelty framing was rewritten against a literature search that distinguishes the 3,4,5-trinitro-1,2-isoxazole from Hervé's published 3,4,5-trinitro-1H-pyrazole (commit 8320754).

## Section 2: Strengths from a chemistry perspective (updated)

The five v1.0 strengths (honest layered surrogate disclosure, hard-negative SMARTS, xTB gap gate, anchor probe set, retrosynthesis honesty) are unchanged. The revision adds three further strengths.

**The ZPE unit-bug discovery is itself a chemistry-credibility win.** That a single PySCF API misread (freq_au as energy rather than angular frequency) was responsible for the 16,763 kJ/mol intercept I flagged as physically implausible is a result I am happy to see published. The pre-fix RDX cached ZPE was 16,013 kJ/mol against an actual 375 kJ/mol; the propagated bias landed on the calibration intercept and was absorbed silently. The authors found it, patched it, re-derived 17 cached per-lead JSONs, and preserved the pre-fix values as audit fields. This is the right way to handle a numerical-bug discovery.

**The post-fix anchor calibration is now plausible.** RDX experimental HOF is +66 to +70 kJ/mol; the post-fix DFT atomization-energy is +240.4 kJ/mol; the regression intercept of -205.7 kJ/mol gives a calibrated RDX HOF of +34.7 kJ/mol, within the 2-anchor LOO residual band of 62.5 kJ/mol. TATB experimental is -141 kJ/mol; post-fix DFT is +82.9 kJ/mol; calibrated -122.8 kJ/mol, again within band. This is a defensible screening-grade calibration. The density half (slope 4.275, L1 calibrated rho = 2.53 g/cm3) is still problematic and is unchanged from v1.0.

**Per-lead h50 numbers from two independent routes.** L1 is now reported as h50 = 30.3 cm (literature-grounded score-model head, score_model_v3e_h50 trained on Huang and Massa) and h50 = 82.7 cm (Politzer and Murray 2014 chemotype-class BDE regression). RDX anchor reads 26.3 cm (model) vs 25-30 cm experimental, which is the right consistency check. TATB anchor reads 89.2 cm (model) vs 140-490 cm experimental, which the authors flag as a known underprediction band rather than glossing over. This addresses my v1.0 §3.4 complaint that no per-lead h50 was reported.

## Section 3: Walk-through of v1.0 Major chemistry concerns

**v1.0 §3.1: Atomization-energy HOF at B3LYP/6-31G(d) is not fit for absolute purpose.**
- v1.0 quote: "The 16,763 kJ/mol calibration intercept is doing all the work, and it is forced through only two anchors with zero residual diagnostic ... atomization-energy HOFs at B3LYP/6-31G(d) without thermal correction, basis-set extrapolation, and atomic-state spin-orbit corrections are not usable as an absolute property predictor."
- v1.1+: The intercept is now -205.7 kJ/mol, which is in the right order of magnitude for the well-known systematic atomization-energy bias on small CHNO heterocycles (the GMTKN55 5-15 kJ/mol per atom band predicts roughly 75-300 kJ/mol on a 15-25 atom molecule). The authors did not run G4 or CBS-QB3 on L1, L4, L5 in revision; the absolute-grade claim therefore remains screening-grade only, which they now acknowledge explicitly throughout §5.5 and Appendix D. The 2-anchor LOO residual band of plus or minus 62.5 kJ/mol on calibrated HOF is reported.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. The intercept is no longer physically implausible. However, the request for composite-method (G4, CBS-QB3) HOFs on the top three leads as an independent validation was not actioned. The paper is now defensible as a screening tool but not as an absolute-grade thermochemistry contribution; the authors agree with this framing in the revised prose, so the dispute is resolved at the level of "what the paper claims it is", which I accept.

**v1.0 §3.2: Kamlet-Jacobs in the high-N CHNO regime is not a publishable absolute-property predictor.**
- v1.0 quote: "K-J ... obtains D = 13.27 km/s for L4 ... a number neither the authors nor any reader would defend ... an EXPLO5 or Cheetah-2 thermochemical-equilibrium recompute on the same calibrated rho and HOF, with full Chapman-Jouguet product-distribution treatment ... is required."
- v1.1+: The L4 = 13.27 km/s number was a direct artefact of the inflated HOF intercept; under the post-fix calibration it reads 7.01 km/s (Table D.2). For L1 the K-J calibrated D = 9.52 km/s now agrees with the 3D-CNN surrogate to 0.04 km/s. The authors built a Cantera ideal-gas CJ root-finder (compute_cj_detonation.py) and report it honestly: absolute D values are roughly 3x too low because no covolume EOS is used at 30-100 GPa product pressures, but the relative L4/RDX ratio (1.01 from Cantera vs 1.61 from K-J calibrated) confirms the high-N regime breakdown is a regression artefact rather than a thermochemical truth. EXPLO5 / Cheetah-2 was not run.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. The implausible L4 = 13.27 km/s number is dispatched. The L1 K-J D is now well-defined and matches the surrogate. EXPLO5 / Cheetah-2 absolute confirmation, which I asked for explicitly, is still missing; the Cantera scaffold is the right partial substitute and the authors are honest about its limits, but it does not close the absolute-D question for L1, L4, L5.

**v1.0 §3.3: Bondi van-der-Waals density with packing 0.69, calibrated by 4.275*rho - 5.172, is alarming.**
- v1.0 quote: "The slope of 4.275 is far outside the range I would expect ... a slope this large means tiny noise in raw rho_DFT ... is amplified onto the final calibrated density. L1 raw 1.80 -> calibrated 2.53 g/cm3 is implausible for an aromatic CHNO."
- v1.1+: The density slope is unchanged. L1 calibrated rho = 2.53 g/cm3 still appears in Table D.1c. No CSP, no Stine-Hofmann group-additivity check, no 6-anchor refit was added in revision. Appendix D.4 still defers the 6-anchor extension. The abstract continues to use the 3D-CNN rho = 2.00 g/cm3 as the headline density, which is more conservative than 2.53 but is a different estimator.
- Verdict: STILL OPEN. This is the single most important chemistry weakness that survived the revision. A 6-anchor density calibration with HMX, PETN, FOX-7, NTO added is approximately 24-72 hours of routine B3LYP/6-31G(d) optimisation plus a regression refit, and would either bring the slope into the 1.0-1.5 range I would accept or confirm the methodology needs to be replaced. The L1 calibrated rho of 2.53 g/cm3, beating CL-20 by 0.5 g/cm3 with no precedent in the experimental literature, cannot stand as written.

**v1.0 §3.4: No impact, friction, ESD, or thermal-decomposition data or prediction for the leads.**
- v1.0 quote: "the absence of any sensitivity prediction is a deal-breaker ... a Table 5b extension where each chem-pass lead carries a predicted h50 (with uncertainty), a predicted DSC onset, and a Mathieu impact-sensitivity score ... would convince me."
- v1.1+: Table 1 and Table D.1c now carry per-lead h50 (cm) from two independent routes: the trained literature-grounded sensitivity head (score_model_v3e_h50, fit on roughly 307 Huang-Massa rows) and a Politzer-Murray chemotype-class BDE regression (h50 = 1.93*BDE - 52.4). Anchor sanity check is reported (RDX 26.3 cm model vs 25-30 cm literature; TATB 89.2 cm model vs 140-490 cm literature, with the latter underprediction flagged). DSC onset and Mathieu impact-sensitivity scores were not added.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. The h50 is the most important of the three sensitivity predictions and it is now in the paper with two independent estimates and an anchor sanity check. DSC onset and Mathieu impact-sensitivity remain absent; these would close the sensitivity-triage layer fully but their absence is no longer a deal-breaker now that h50 is reported.

**v1.0 §3.5: Trinitro-1,2-isoxazole (L1) specifically is essentially Hervé-group rediscovery, novelty overstated.**
- v1.0 quote: "L1 is essentially an extension of a well-known Hervé-group scaffold ... the contribution should be presented as rediscovery of a sensible chemotype, not as a new energetic candidate."
- v1.1+: §1, §1.5, §6, §8 and the abstract were rewritten (commit 8320754) following an explicit literature search across PubChem, RSC, ACS, ScienceDirect, Wiley journals. The honest finding is that 3,4,5-trinitro-1,2-isoxazole as a discrete molecule does not appear in the open energetic-materials literature; Hervé and Roussel's 2010 Angew. Chem. paper is on 3,4,5-trinitro-1H-pyrazole (the isomeric pyrazole, NOT the isoxazole), and partial-decoration isoxazole derivatives (4,5-dinitroisoxazole, side-group trinitromethyl-isoxazoles) are published but not the fully-substituted L1 ring. The paper now frames L1 as a rediscovery of the polynitroisoxazole chemotype family rather than as a new chemotype.
- Verdict: RESOLVED. The reframing is the right one and the literature attribution to Hervé / Sheremetev is correctly placed on the isomeric pyrazole and on partial-decoration isoxazoles, not on L1 directly. ref-herve2010 is now in the bibliography.

**v1.0 §3.6: Retrosynthetic auditing - L1 route quality and L4/L5 nullity.**
- v1.0 quote: "the route is plausible but the final step is the dangerous one and is exactly where bench yield typically collapses ... the ZINC catalog records virtual catalog availability, not bench availability ... a targeted literature search by the authors ... would have produced credible routes for L4 and L5."
- v1.1+: §5.6 was rewritten against the actual aizynth_results.json content (commit 7482920): n_routes = 9, state_score = 0.50, step_count = 4, with explicit acknowledgement that the four energetic-domain intermediates are NOT bench-available (only tert-butanol and DPPA are). The wet-lab framing was downgraded from "most actionable" to "most template-reachable target ... requires sourcing of the energetic-domain intermediates that ZINC does not stock". An AiZynth extension run on the other 9 chem-pass leads (L2, L3, L9, L11, L13, L16, L18, L19, L20) is reported in Table 9b: zero productive routes within the 200-MCTS / 300-s budget, attributed correctly to USPTO-template MCTS bias against energetic-domain ring closures (N2O5 in CH2Cl2 with dehydrating agent, NO2BF4 nitrations, Ac2O/HNO3 oxime nitrations). Hand-drawn forward routes from the energetic-materials literature for L4 and L5 were not added.
- Verdict: PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. The L1 route audit and the ZINC-vs-bench distinction are resolved. The L4 / L5 hand-drawn forward route from the energetic-materials literature was not added; the paper continues to say AiZynth returns null on these leads with an honest USPTO-bias caveat, but no positive forward-route claim is made. This is acceptable as a candidate-grade paper but a follow-up note in §5.6 citing N2O5 / NO2BF4 nitramine ring-closure precedent and Ac2O/HNO3 oxime-nitrate precedent would close the chemistry side of this concern.

## Section 5: Specific questions for the authors (updated)

1. The 6-anchor density refit (HMX, PETN, FOX-7, NTO added) was deferred again. The L1 calibrated rho = 2.53 g/cm3 cannot stand. Will the 6-anchor extension land in the next revision pass?
2. EXPLO5 / Cheetah-2 academic licences are free. Why was Cantera (which the authors themselves flag as ideal-gas-EOS-only and 3x too low in absolute terms) chosen as the only thermochemical replacement, rather than EXPLO5 in parallel?
3. G4 / CBS-QB3 single-points on L1, L4, L5, RDX, TATB are 6-24 CPU-hours each. Why were these not run during the revision cycle, given that the calibration's absolute-grade defensibility hinges on them?
4. L9 and L20 are still reported with minimum real frequency 0 cm-1 in Table D.1. Either these geometries are not minima (in which case the leads must be removed) or this is a printing artefact. Which is it?
5. The Politzer-Murray chemotype-class BDE regression gives L1 h50 = 82.7 cm and the trained sensitivity head gives 30.3 cm. That is a factor-of-2.7 disagreement on the headline lead. What is the reader supposed to take away?
6. For L4 (tetrazoline-N-nitramine), the energetics-literature N2O5 / NO2BF4 ring-closure precedent is well-known. Will a hand-drawn forward route be cited in §5.6, or will L4 stand on the AiZynth-null result alone?

## Section 6: Decision recommendation to the editor

**Minor revision.**

The two most damaging v1.0 chemistry findings (atomization-energy HOF intercept of -16,763 kJ/mol; K-J L4 = 13.27 km/s and K-J undefined for L1) were both downstream of one ZPE unit bug, which has been patched. The post-fix L1 K-J D = 9.52 km/s now agrees with the 3D-CNN surrogate to 0.04 km/s and the L4 number is 7.01 km/s, in line with what a high-N CHNO compound should read on K-J. The Hervé-pyrazole vs L1-isoxazole literature attribution has been corrected with a real cross-database literature search (commit 8320754). Per-lead h50 sensitivity is now in Table 1 with an anchor sanity check on RDX and TATB.

What survives the revision is one important chemistry concern and a handful of minor items. The 6-anchor density calibration was deferred again, and the L1 calibrated rho = 2.53 g/cm3 still beats CL-20 by 0.5 g/cm3 with no precedent in the experimental literature; this is the single most important chemistry follow-up. EXPLO5 / Cheetah-2 absolute D for L1, L4, L5 was not run; the Cantera scaffold is the right honest partial substitute but does not close this question. G4 / CBS-QB3 composite-method HOF on the top three leads was not run. L9 / L20 minimum real frequency 0 cm-1 was not fixed.

If the authors come back with (a) a 6-anchor density calibration with reported slope, intercept, and LOO residual; (b) the L9 / L20 imaginary-frequency clarification; and (c) at least one of EXPLO5 detonation or G4 HOF on the top three leads, I would recommend acceptance. The methodology contribution is strong and the chemistry honesty is now well above the median ML-for-energetics submission. The L1 candidate is at "credible screening-grade with a documented density-calibration uncertainty band", which is a fair description for an NMI-level methodology paper provided the density-calibration limitation is foregrounded in the abstract. As things stand the abstract still uses the 3D-CNN rho = 2.00, which is the conservative number and is now buttressed by the K-J calibrated D = 9.52 km/s; this is acceptable provided the limitation is honest in §5.5 and Appendix D.

## Section 7: Confidential comments to the editor

The v1.0 to v1.1+ revision is one of the cleaner numerical-bug responses I have read in this domain. The ZPE unit-error find is the kind of root-cause discovery that turns multiple reviewer complaints (mine and the ML reviewer's) into a single fix, and the audit-trail (*_pre_unit_fix fields, m2_calibration.json updated with LOO residual, Cantera independent confirmation that L4 = 13.27 km/s was an artefact) is exactly what I want to see. The remaining 6-anchor density calibration is the one chemistry follow-up I would not waive; if the authors will not run it, the calibrated rho column in Table D.1c should be removed and only the 3D-CNN rho reported. I would lean accept-with-minor-revision, with the density-calibration follow-up as the single decisive item for the next round.
